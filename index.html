About this strategy ▾

The base trade

A long-call directional bet on QQQ: each Friday, buy one ~30-delta call around 60 days to expiration and exit at 45 DTE, with a stop at 75% of premium. One active trade at a time. This calculator layers a sizing-and-filter overlay on top of that base thesis.

What the backtest revealed

Over 100 trades (2021–2026) the base strategy made +$7,927 at a 42% win rate. But the shape of that profit is the whole point: the top 5 trades produced ~81% of all gains. This is a fat-right-tail strategy — you lose small and often, and a handful of trades that catch a strong QQQ rally carry everything.

That single fact drives every rule. QQQ price move and trade P/L correlate at 0.91 — it's almost a pure directional bet, so the job is to be large when conditions favor a runner and small when they don't, and to never cap the winners.

Why these specific rules

Go / No-Go: The only filter that removed losers without sacrificing many winners was skipping when QQQ is both overbought (RSI > 65) and extended (+20-day > 4%) — buying short-dated calls into a frothy, stretched rally was the single worst entry.

Size up on weakness: Entries below the 50-day MA or after a negative 20-day return averaged far higher expectancy than entries into strength. Counterintuitive, but the data was clear — buying dips beat chasing momentum.

Streak override: After three straight wins the next trade averaged −$37; after a cold run it averaged +$260. So hot streaks cut size, cold streaks add it — the opposite of instinct.

Hold to 45 DTE, never take profit early: Simulating a profit target at +100% of premium collapsed +$7,927 down to roughly break-even. Fixed targets amputate the exact right-tail runners the strategy depends on. The only valid early exit is thesis invalidation — QQQ breaking its trend on real volume.

Risk cap: Because losses cluster (the backtest showed a 73.82% peak drawdown), no single trade should risk more than ~2–3% of equity. Sizing rules reinforce that ceiling rather than override it.

The net effect

Applying the filter + sizing to the same 100 trades produced +$11,641 versus +$7,927 flat — about +47%. The gain came almost entirely from sizing up on the good setups, not from skipping the bad ones. The tradeoff: bigger positions also meant a larger drawdown, so the extra return is not free.

Honest limits

This rests on ~100 trades over one mostly-bullish era, with indicators reconstructed from interpolated weekly prices — so the exact thresholds (RSI 65, +4%, 1.75×) are informed starting points, not validated constants. A proper backtest on real daily QQQ and options data would refine them. Treat the outputs as a disciplined framework, not a guarantee.
